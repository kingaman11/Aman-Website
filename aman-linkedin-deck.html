Roku Pay Guardian · RPG

Two agents.
Zero approvals.
Always on.

From operation to optimization.

Today, every step is manual — flag, failover, impact analysis. A small bank block hides under the noise until impact grows. Aman scans, hands off, Eng edits, Vicky measures — every step by hand.

RPG changes that. A fully autonomous loop where humans get the post-fact summary — no gates, no paging, no meetings.

Problem
Scan → alert → draft failover → impact analysis — all by hand.
Approach
Detect, ship, analyze — every hour, autonomously.
Outcome
Humans approve nothing. RPG keeps optimizing without a meeting.
STEP 01

Continuous canary

When a primary PSP dips below target, RPG sends 1% of traffic to an alternate to learn what's possible.

STEP 02

Real comparison

Hold the probe long enough to be statistically significant — never act on noise, never overreact to a bad hour.

STEP 03

Auto-promote

Winner becomes new primary. Loser becomes the next probe. RPG keeps optimizing without a meeting.

How I Operate
12 / 16

Four principles I work by.

The operating system behind every initiative, dashboard, and decision.

01

Instrument first, then optimize.

If you can't see it, you can't fix it. Every problem starts with making the system observable — dashboards, alerts, and metrics before strategy.

02

Automate the boring before the brilliant.

The biggest wins come from removing manual toil — not chasing exotic ideas. A 90% reporting-time reduction frees the team to think strategically.

03

Let the data write the meeting.

Decisions should come from instrumented truth, not opinion theater. Build analysis so the next step is obvious — and the next meeting shorter.

04

Ship in canaries. Promote on evidence.

From RPG's 1% probe-and-promote loop to Tap to Pay rollouts — never bet the system on an untested theory. Small experiments compound.
